# FOUNDATION — FORMAL FRAMEWORK OF MATRIX THEORY (MT)
## Strict Academic Version

This document defines MT's scope, fundamental operators, and the deterministic principles of Universe evolution.

---

## 1. SCOPE

MT describes transitions between Horizontals within a single Vertical energy cycle. The operational range spans scales from \(10^{-115}\) m to \(10^{105}\) m — determined by Qn resolution and H+n modulation periods. The number of Verticals in Eternity is outside MT's scope.

## 3. VERTICAL AND HORIZONTALS

**Vertical** \( \mathcal{V} = \{ E_{H-3}, ..., E_{H-\text{min}} \} \) supplies continuous energy flow to H0 via \( \mathcal{P}_{L1} \). **Horizontal** \( H_n \) is a stable energy matrix.

Eternity's cycle is characterized by a periodic energy transfer operator between Vertical and Horizontals; its endpoints are undefined.

---

## 4. H0 EVOLUTION

H0 evolution is a sequence of spatial expansion steps triggered by \( \rho_{\mathcal{V}} > \rho_{\text{krit}} \). Transition time:
\[
t_{\text{expansion}} \propto \frac{1}{\rho_{\mathcal{V}} - \rho_{\text{krit}}}
\]
Consequences: new Qn structure, broken molecular bonds, Oort cloud formation, star rebirth.

## 5. L1 ZONE AND CMB

## 6. MATRIX ARCHITECTURE

H0 geometry is static; energy content is dynamic (Vertical pyramid + H+n modulations). Photon energy loss:
\[
\frac{dE}{dx} = -\beta \cdot E \cdot \frac{\rho_{\mathcal{V}}}{\rho_{\text{H0}}}
