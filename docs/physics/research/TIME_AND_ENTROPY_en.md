## 1. CLASSICAL PROBLEM — SUMMARY

In classical physics, time is:

- **Newtonian:** absolute, uniformly flowing, independent of everything.
- **Einsteinian:** relative, dependent on speed and gravity, part of spacetime.
- **Quantum mechanics:** a parameter in which the wave function evolves, but not explained.

Entropy in classical physics is:

- **Thermodynamics:** a measure of disorder that always increases (second law).
- **Statistical physics:** the logarithm of the number of microstates of a system.
- **Cosmology:** the entropy of the Universe increases, but it is not explained why it was initially low.

**Problems:**

1. Time is not connected to energy — it is an external parameter, not a physical property.
2. There is no explanation for why time has an arrow (one-way flow).
3. Entropy increase is interpreted as "increase in disorder," but it is unclear why the Universe was initially so ordered.
4. There is no explanation for the connection between time and gravity/quantum effects.

**Time is not the speed of the clock.** Time is the **number of transfer steps** the clock has completed.

\[

- Therefore, a process (e.g., an atom) appears slower — but time itself has not changed.
