# QUANTUM MECHANICS — COMPARISON WITH MT
## Strict Academic Version

This document compares classical quantum mechanics and MT interpretations using only operational terms.

---

## 1. WAVE–PARTICLE DUALITY

**Classical QM**: duality considered fundamental.

**MT**: particle (proton, electron) corresponds to a closed TE flow loop; wave corresponds to an open TE flow. This difference is structural, not ontological — determined by flow circulation boundary conditions.

---

## 2. WAVE FUNCTION AND BORN RULE

**Classical QM**: \( \psi \) is probability amplitude, \( |\psi|^2 \) probability density.

**MT**: \( \psi(\mathbf{x}, t) \sim e^{i\theta(\mathbf{x}, t)} \), where \( \theta \) is ID1 phase. \( |\psi|^2 \) is the projected TE flow density:

\[
|\psi|^2 = \frac{\Phi(\mathbf{x}, t)}{\Phi_0}
\]

Born's rule follows from a matrix structural constraint — flow is discrete and its density expressible only as a quadratic form.

## 3. SUPERPOSITION AND INTERFERENCE

**Classical QM**: linear combination.

**MT**: Superposition corresponds to TE flow distribution over multiple Qn channels. Interference arises from phase differences:

\[
\psi_{\text{tot}} = \sum_i e^{i\theta_i} \cdot \Phi_i
\]

The L0 background ensures channel coherence.

## 4. MEASUREMENT PROBLEM

**Classical QM**: wave function collapse.

**MT**: Measurement is a phase selection operator \( \mathcal{P}_{L1} \) that fixes \( \theta \) at a given Qn point. No collapse occurs — only projection onto L1.

---

## 5. UNCERTAINTY PRINCIPLE

**Classical QM**: \( \Delta x \cdot \Delta p \geq \hbar/2 \), fundamental.

**MT**: Position (Qn) and momentum (FV) are mutually orthogonal coordinates. If phase \( \theta \) is known with precision \( \Delta \theta \):

\[
\Delta x \cdot \Delta p = \frac{\hbar}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{\sin(\Delta \theta)}
\]

As \( \Delta \theta \to 0 \), uncertainty vanishes — it is a resolution limit of the measuring device, not a fundamental property.

**MT**: two entanglement types:
- **Type 1** — via shared Qn structure, propagates at light speed.
- **Type 2** — via TZ–Vertical–TZ, instantaneous but without signal transfer (momentum transfer).
