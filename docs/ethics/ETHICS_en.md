## 6. HUMAN RESPONSIBILITY — PRACTICAL RECOMMENDATIONS (ID-1 / ID0 / ID1.4) — 3.0

**MT technologies require responsibility because they are dangerous (ID-1 / ID0).**

- They operate beyond H0 limitations (ID-1) — they are harder to control.

- They require deep understanding of the Vertical's regularities (ID-1) — without it, they are dangerous.

This means that before implementing any type 2 technology, there must be full understanding of \( \rho_{\mathcal{V}} \) dynamics and its influence on \( \varepsilon_0 \), \( \mu_0 \), and \( G \).

This means that type 2 technologies must be **passive** — they must respond to Vertical signals, not actively change \( \rho_{\mathcal{V}} \).

**According to ROADMAP 3.0:** Responsible technologies are those that operate only in the L1 path (use existing \( \rho_{\mathcal{V}} \) projection), not attempting to change the H0 path (actively disrupt \( \rho_{\mathcal{V}} \)).
