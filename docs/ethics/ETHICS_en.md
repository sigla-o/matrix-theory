## 5. BALANCE WITH THE VERTICAL — QUANTITATIVE CRITERIA (ID-1 / ID0)

**Technologies must be in balance with the Vertical (ID-1) — they must use the energy flow, not release an explosion.**

### 5.1. What does "in balance" mean? — quantitative indicators (ID-1 / ID0)

**Not "take," but "connect."** Use the energy flow that already exists (ID-1 / ID0), not tear it out.

Quantitative criterion:
\[
\frac{\Delta \rho_{\mathcal{V}}}{\rho_{\mathcal{V}}^{(0)}} \ll 1
\]
— the technology must not change Vertical energy density (ID-1) by more than a small fraction.

**Not explosion, but sustainability.** The technology must be sustainable — it can operate continuously without disturbing Vertical balance (ID-1).

Quantitative criterion:
\[
\frac{d}{dt} \rho_{\mathcal{V}} \approx 0
\]
— the system must maintain equilibrium over time (ID-1 / ID0).

**Not incompatibility, but compatibility.** The technology must be compatible with the H0 matrix (ID0) — it exists within the matrix, not destroying it.

Quantitative criterion:
\[
\Delta \Phi(\mathbf{x}) < \Phi_{\text{krit}}
\]
— the local disturbance of TE flow (ID0) must not exceed the critical threshold.

### 5.2. Why is "balance" more important than "control"? (ID-1 / ID0)

- The Vertical is an energy accumulator (ID-1) that exceeds H0 regularities (ID0).
- A person attempting to control it encounters forces they cannot model or predict (ID-1 / ID0).
- **Control** is an illusion — **balance** is real (ID-1 / ID0).
