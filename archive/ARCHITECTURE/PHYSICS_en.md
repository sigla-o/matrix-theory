# PHYSICS

## Purpose

PHYSICS defines how the CORE principles are realized within physical organization.

This document is not a complete description of physics. It establishes the universal principles from which every physical object and process described by the theory is generated.

---

# 1. Principle of Physical Organization

Physical objects are not the starting point of the theory.

They are the result of energy organization.

Every physical object emerges through successive increases in organizational complexity within the available energy balance.

---

# 2. Horizontals

Each Horizontal is characterized by:

- its own matrix type;
- its own energy balance;
- its own organizational laws.

Horizontals do not represent separate theories.

They are different organizational levels of the same Vertical.

---

# 3. Evolution

Organization develops sequentially.

Each evolutionary level uses the previously achieved organization as its foundation.

No evolutionary level develops in isolation.

---

# 4. Organizational Scale

The same organizational principle operates at every scale.

Examples include:

- electromagnetic cycles;
- matrix points;
- protons;
- atoms;
- chemical elements;
- molecules;
- macroscopic objects.

Only the organizational scale changes.

---

# 5. Universal Coordinate System

Every physical object possesses a coordinate.

The coordinate defines:

- the matrix type;
- the organizational level;
- the organizational position.

The object's name is an interpretation of its coordinate.

---

# 6. Organizational Constraints

No physical system can exceed:

- the energy balance of its Horizontal;
- its evolutionary level;
- its organizational coordinate.

These constraints define the limits of physical organization.

---

# 7. Universal Applicability

The PHYSICS principles apply equally to:

- elementary particles;
- atoms;
- chemical elements;
- molecules;
- materials;
- astronomical objects.

Only the organizational scale changes.
