## 4. Gravity mechanism — channel blocking and refilling

### 4.1. Background

TE VEU H-2 flow continuously flows through the H0 matrix. Its FV changes in a 360° cycle determined by ID1 rotation.

### 4.2. Obstacle (source)

A proton (or any matter object) occupies a certain H0 space. In this space, TE flow **cannot pass through** — the proton blocks it.

**One proton blocks approximately 10²⁰ TE VEU H-2 channels.**

### 4.3. Rarified zone (shadow)

- If the FV cycle is divided into individual "frames", each frame shows a specific flow direction.
- The proton blocks those channels that in this frame pass through its occupied region.
- These channels remain **unfilled with TE** — creating a local pressure rarefaction.

### 4.4. Refilling (FV's role)

- If the flow were only unidirectional, the empty channels would continue their motion indefinitely — no gravity would exist.
- **FV** (360° cycle) ensures that **nearly parallel channels from the surroundings refill the missing TE**.
- The refilling capacity is **constant** — determined by FV dynamics.
- What changes is **the number of unfilled channels** — the farther from the source, the fewer channels remain empty.

### 4.5. 1/r² — not a law, but geometry

**1/r² is not a "force law". It is the geometric reduction in the number of unfilled channels.**

- `r = 1`: 10²⁰ unfilled channels.
- `r = 2`: 10²⁰ / 4 = 2.5 × 10¹⁹ unfilled channels.
- `r = 3`: 10²⁰ / 9 ≈ 1.11 × 10¹⁹ unfilled channels.
- ...

The greater the distance from the source, the fewer channels the proton can cover — the fewer channels remain unfilled. This is not "force weakening", but a **geometric shrinkage of the source's sphere of influence**.

## 5. Gravitational force — pressure difference

**Gravity is the TE pressure difference on objects.**

- The number of unfilled channels creates a local pressure rarefaction.
- Another object located in this rarefied zone experiences a pressure difference: on one side it is pushed by full TE flow, on the other by rarefaction.
- This pressure difference **pushes** it toward the rarefaction.

**Gravity is a force that can be calculated as the TE pressure difference on the objects it affects.**

**Conclusion:**
A gravitational wave is the same as an EM wave, only larger — an H-2 energy density fluctuation that acts on matter.
