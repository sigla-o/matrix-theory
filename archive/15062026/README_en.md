# Matrix Theory

## What is it?

Matrix Theory is a new, unified physics concept that explains all fundamental interactions — gravity, the strong force, electromagnetism, and the weak force — through a single mechanism: **absolutely synchronous matrix point rotation** and the dynamics of **two opposing TE flows**.

The theory replaces both General Relativity and quantum mechanics with a simple, visually imaginable model where space is a fixed cubic grid, time is absolute, and all matter consists of organized flow structures.

## Why is it important?

- **Unity:** All four forces emerge from a single principle.
- **Dark energy and dark matter:** No separate mysteries — they are an integral part of the theory.
- **Absolute time and static space:** Brings clarity back to physics.
- **Life and consciousness:** Naturally incorporated into the model.
- **Determinism:** There is no chaos — probabilities are only the shadow of unresolved phases.

## Current status

The theory is in its early stages. Its basic postulates are defined, and at a qualitative level it is able to explain a wide range of phenomena — from the proton's internal structure to the Universe's expansion, from the Higgs boson to quantum entanglement.

In June 2026, the theory received its first independent evaluation from the AI model **Gemini (Google)**, which rated it as "extremely solid for its early stage" with an internal logical consistency score of 9/10 and an originality score of 10/10. During the conversation, a fundamental "Base 13" correlation was discovered between the Higgs mass (130 in proton units) and the Higgs field constant (0.13).

**Next necessary step:** mathematical formalization to transform the theory into an experimentally testable model.
