## 5. BALANCE WITH THE VERTICAL — QUANTITATIVE CRITERIA

**Technologies must be in balance with the Vertical — they must use energy flow, not unleash explosions.**

### 5.1. What does "in balance" mean? — quantitative indicators

**Not "taking", but "connecting".** Use the energy flow that already exists, rather than tearing it out.

Quantitative criterion:
\[
\frac{\Delta \rho_{\mathcal{V}}}{\rho_{\mathcal{V}}^{(0)}} \ll 1
\]
— technology must not change Vertical energy density by more than a small fraction.

**Not explosion, but sustainability.** Technology must be sustainable — it can operate continuously without disturbing the Vertical balance.

Quantitative criterion:
\[
\frac{d}{dt} \rho_{\mathcal{V}} \approx 0
\]
— the system must maintain equilibrium over time.

**Not incompatibility, but compatibility.** Technology must be compatible with the H0 matrix — it exists within the matrix, not destroying it.

Quantitative criterion:
\[
\Delta \Phi(\mathbf{x}) < \Phi_{\text{crit}}
\]
— local TE flow disturbance must not exceed the critical threshold.

### 5.2. Why "balance" is more important than "control"?

- The Vertical is an energy accumulator that exceeds H0 regularities.
- A person attempting to control it encounters forces they cannot model or predict.
- **Control** is an illusion — **balance** is real.

## 6. HUMAN RESPONSIBILITY — PRACTICAL RECOMMENDATIONS

**MT technologies require responsibility because they are dangerous.**

- They operate beyond H0 limitations — they are harder to control.
- They can be used for both good and evil — like nuclear physics.
- They require deep understanding of the Vertical's principles — without it, they are dangerous.

1. **Understand, not create.** We cannot create TZ, but we can understand its principles.
2. **Use existing channels.** Life already uses type 2 (TZ–Vertical–TZ). Technologies should imitate this mechanism, not try to replace it.
3. **Respect boundaries.** The TZ is not a tool — it is a boundary. Those who cross it face consequences they cannot control.
