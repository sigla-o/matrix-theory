# CORE

## Purpose

CORE contains the immutable generative principles of Matrix Theory.

This document is not intended to describe physical objects or processes. It defines the fundamental axioms upon which all other Architecture documents and the public theory are built.

---

# 1. Eternity

Eternity is the global energy cycle.

Matrix Theory does not attempt to describe the complete structure of Eternity and does not seek to determine how many Verticals exist within it.

The theory analyzes a single Vertical.

---

# 2. Vertical

A Vertical is a local energy cycle within Eternity.

The formation of a Vertical begins in the Fundamental Horizontal of the Eternity cycle.

The organizational processes within the Fundamental Horizontal are analogous to those occurring in H0, but operate at different energetic and spatial scales.

Anything beyond the Fundamental Horizontal lies outside the scope of the current theory.

---

# 3. Horizontals

A Vertical consists of Horizontals.

Horizontals represent the energetic levels of a Vertical.

Each Horizontal is defined by its available energy balance and the corresponding limit of organizational complexity.

---

# 4. Energy Organization

Energy alone does not determine organization.

Energy accumulation creates an energy balance.

The available energy balance determines the maximum possible level of organization.

The process follows the sequence:

```text
Energy
      ↓
Energy accumulation
      ↓
Energy balance
      ↓
Organization
      ↓
Evolution
```

No organization can exceed its available energy balance.

---

# 5. Hierarchy of Energy Balances

Energy balances form a hierarchical structure.

```text
Eternity
    ↓
Vertical
    ↓
Horizontals
    ↓
Subsequent organizational levels
```

The total balance of every internal system is always part of the balance of its enclosing system.

---

# 6. Sphere-within-Sphere Principle

Energy organizes itself into closed energy balances.

Within Matrix Theory, such a balance is referred to as a "sphere".

A sphere is not a geometric object.

It represents a closed energy balance.

Lower-level spheres may form within higher-level spheres.

Their maximum available energy balance is always limited by the balance of the enclosing sphere.

---

# 7. Universal Organization Principle

The same organizational principle operates at every level of the theory.

The following may change:

- energetic scale;
- spatial scale;
- organizational complexity.

The following remain invariant:

- the principle of energy accumulation;
- the principle of energy balance;
- the principle of organization.

Together they form the universal generative core of Matrix Theory.
