# FOUNDATION

## Purpose

FOUNDATION defines the scope of Matrix Theory.

It describes what the theory models, where its reconstruction begins, and where its scope ends.

FOUNDATION does not describe physical processes. It establishes the starting point of the theory.

---

# 1. Scope of the Theory

Matrix Theory does not attempt to describe all of reality.

It describes a single Vertical within the energy cycle of Eternity.

Questions concerning how many Verticals exist in Eternity or how they are organized are outside the scope of the theory.

---

# 2. Eternity

Eternity is the global energy cycle.

It is the highest organizational level used by the theory as its reference framework.

The complete structure of Eternity is not modeled.

---

# 3. Fundamental Horizontal

The formation of every Vertical begins within the Fundamental Horizontal.

The Fundamental Horizontal is the initial environment for energy accumulation.

Its organizational principles are analogous to those of H0, while operating at different energetic and spatial scales.

### Coordinate System of Matrix Evolution

In Matrix Theory, evolution is described by two mutually orthogonal coordinate axes: Vertical (H) and Horizontal (Q). Each axis represents a distinct evolutionary process and cannot be reduced to the other.

**Vertical Evolution (H)** describes the sequential transition between Horizontals. Its direction extends from H+max toward H-min, where H0 corresponds to the observable Universe. Vertical Evolution is always directed toward increasingly finer energy organization, since finer organization provides greater stability and a higher probability of long-term energy-object existence.

Each H level is a complete Horizontal containing its own independent Horizontal evolutionary process. Transition to the next Vertical level is possible only after complete energy balance has been achieved within the corresponding Horizontal.

**Horizontal Evolution (Q)** occurs at a constant H level (H = const) and describes local organizational development from Q1 to Qmax. It determines the structural complexity of objects within a given Horizontal but does not directly alter the identity of the Vertical Evolution Unit (VEU).

From the perspective of Matrix Theory, H and Q together form a unified coordinate system of evolution. From the perspective of Eternity, however, the entire Matrix is only a local evolutionary process. Eternity is not a part of Vertical Evolution and cannot be identified with H+max. Instead, it is the higher-order context within which Matrix evolution unfolds as one process of the Eternity Cycle.

---

# 4. Formation of a Vertical

Energy accumulation within the Fundamental Horizontal initiates the formation of a Vertical.

As the Vertical develops, successive Horizontals emerge, each defined by its available energy balance.

The theory does not describe any process preceding the Fundamental Horizontal.

---

# 5. Horizontals

Horizontals form the organizational structure of a Vertical.

Each Horizontal represents a specific level of energy balance and organization.

H0 is one of the Horizontals.

It is neither the center nor the beginning of the Vertical.

---

# 6. Life

Within Matrix Theory, Life is not treated as a direct stage of object evolution.

The life form — the Soul — belongs to the organization of the Vertical and interacts with the physical organization of H0.

A detailed description of Life is provided in dedicated sections of the theory.

---

# 7. Reconstruction Starting Point

All subsequent reconstruction of the theory begins from the following anchors:

```text
Eternity
      ↓
Fundamental Horizontal
      ↓
Energy Accumulation
      ↓
Formation of the Vertical
      ↓
Horizontals
      ↓
H0
      ↓
Subsequent Organization
```

This sequence defines the generative starting point of Matrix Theory.
