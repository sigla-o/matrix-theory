# META

## Purpose

META defines the interpretation and reconstruction principles of Matrix Theory.

Unlike CORE, which defines the fundamental principles of the theory, META defines how those principles are interpreted, structured, and applied during reconstruction.

META introduces no physical principles.

---

# 1. Reconstruction Principle

Matrix Theory is never reconstructed from objects.

Reconstruction always begins with organizational principles.

Objects are treated as results of organization rather than the starting point of the theory.

Reconstruction sequence:

```text
CORE
      ↓
Energy Balance
      ↓
Organization
      ↓
Coordinate System
      ↓
Objects
      ↓
Names
```

---

# 2. Coordinate Principle

Every object possesses a unique coordinate within the theory.

The coordinate is the object's primary identity.

The object's name is a secondary human-readable label.

If the coordinate is known, the object is completely identified regardless of its name.

---

# 3. Universal Balance Template

All organizational levels are described using a single universal energy balance template.

Fundamental principles:

- the organizational level occupies the center;
- the left side represents the higher-level energy balance;
- the right side represents the evolution of the system itself;
- both sides always remain energetically balanced.

The template is universal and applicable at every scale of the theory.

---

# 4. Hierarchical Interpretation

The same organizational logic operates at every scale.

The following may vary:

- energetic scale;
- spatial scale;
- organizational complexity.

The following remain invariant:

- organization principle;
- energy balance principle;
- coordinate interpretation principle.

---

# 5. Matrix Type

A Horizontal defines the matrix type.

The matrix type defines the organizational environment in which an object exists.

Different matrix types may share identical organizational logic while operating at different energetic levels.

---

# 6. Coordinate Interpretation

Coordinates are interpreted according to unified principles.

Example:

```text
H-1 / ID2,2
```

Interpretation:

- H-1 defines the matrix type;
- ID2 defines the organizational level;
- the additional coordinate specifies the exact organizational position.

The coordinate syntax may evolve without changing the interpretation principle.

---

# 7. Universal Applicability

The universal balance template applies to every organizational scale.

Examples include:

- Verticals;
- Horizontals;
- electromagnetic cycles;
- protons;
- atoms;
- the Periodic Table;
- matrix points.

Only the organizational scale changes.

---

# 8. Reconstruction Consistency

Every new theoretical concept is analyzed in the following order:

1. CORE.
2. META.
3. PHYSICS.
4. Public Theory.
5. AI Framework.

Information must never bypass a higher architectural layer.

This guarantees the internal consistency of the entire theory.
